# BIM4009_SB
Dissecting the HuR–Eltrombopag ‘Dyad’: The role of AU-rich elements in their individual and joint effect
Abstract— What must be present for RNA-binding proteins (RBP) to stabilize their target mRNAs? How do small molecules interfere with their gene regulation? HuR, a ubiquitously expressed RBP, protects mRNAs from degradation by binding AU-rich elements (AREs) in the 3’ UTR. Yet, the extent to which HuR selectively stabilizes ARE-containing (ARE+) transcripts across the transcriptome has not been fully established. Eltrombopag, a small drug used to treat immune thrombocytopenia purpura, has recently been shown to disrupt HuR binding AREs, potentially altering its mRNA-stabilizing function. This study investigates the HuR-Eltrombopag ‘dyad’ across three aims. First, we tested whether HuR selectively stabilizes ARE+ mRNAs across HuR wild-type and mutant conditions. Second, we examined whether Eltrombopag mirrors the effect of HuR loss on ARE+ mRNA abundance and lastly, whether the dyad jointly modulates a subset of mRNAs beyond their independent effects. To do so, we re-analyzed transcriptomic data from four HeLa cell conditions using improved bioinformatic tools. We found that HuR does not selectively stabilize ARE+ transcripts under basal conditions, but Eltrombopag selectively enhances HuR's affinity toward ARE+ transcripts, an effect absent without drug treatment. Furthermore, HuR status was found to affect the direction rather than merely the magnitude of Eltrombopag's transcriptomic response, with direction-reversing drug effects predominating among the top interaction genes. Together, these studies offer key insights into how HuR and Eltrombopag interact within the transcriptome with respect to AREs, advancing our understanding of this dyad in the context of biomedical science and post-transcriptional gene regulation.
Keywords— HuR (ELAVL1), Eltrombopag, AU-Rich Elements, Post-transcriptional regulation, Transcriptomics
